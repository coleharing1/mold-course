## Understanding Mycotoxin Binders

Mycotoxins are fat-soluble toxins that your body struggles to eliminate naturally. They undergo enterohepatic recirculation—meaning they're released from fat cells, processed by the liver into bile, dumped into your intestines, then reabsorbed back into your bloodstream. This cycle continues indefinitely unless interrupted.

**Binders break this cycle** by capturing mycotoxins in your gut before they can be reabsorbed, allowing them to be eliminated through bowel movements.

## Cholestyramine (CSM) - The Gold Standard

<VisualDiagram type="binder-schedule" />

### What is CSM?

Cholestyramine (brand names: Questran, Prevalite) is a prescription bile acid sequestrant originally developed for cholesterol management. Dr. Ritchie Shoemaker discovered it effectively binds mycotoxins and has made it the cornerstone of mold illness treatment.

### How CSM Works

1. **Binding Action**: CSM is a positively charged resin that attracts negatively charged mycotoxins
2. **Non-Absorbed**: CSM stays in your gut—it never enters your bloodstream
3. **Broad Spectrum**: Binds ochratoxin, aflatoxin, trichothecenes, and other mycotoxins
4. **Evidence-Based**: Thousands of patients successfully treated in published studies

## CSM + Welchol Combination Protocol

For those who can't tolerate full-dose CSM, combining with Welchol often improves tolerance:

### The Combination Approach

**Benefits:**
- Better GI tolerance
- Less constipation
- Broader binding spectrum
- Easier titration

**Protocol:**
- Morning: ½ scoop CSM
- Noon: 3 pills Welchol
- Evening: ½ scoop CSM
- Bedtime: 3 pills Welchol

**Equivalency:** 6 Welchol pills = approximately 1 scoop CSM

## Managing Common Side Effects

### Constipation (Most Common)

**Prevention:**
- Increase water intake to half body weight in ounces
- Take 400-800mg magnesium citrate at bedtime
- Add 2 tbsp ground flax or chia seeds daily
- Consider vitamin C to bowel tolerance

**If Severe:**
- Reduce CSM dose temporarily
- Add Smooth Move tea or senna
- Consider coffee enemas (with practitioner guidance)
- May need to alternate CSM days

### Bloating and Gas

**Management:**
- Take with more water
- Digestive enzymes with meals
- Probiotics (2 hours from CSM)
- Ginger or peppermint tea

### Nausea

**Solutions:**
- Mix with apple juice (small amount)
- Take with crackers
- Split dose into smaller amounts
- Consider Welchol instead

### Fatigue (Die-off)

**Indicates:**
- Binders are working
- Toxins being mobilized
- May need to slow down

**Response:**
- Reduce dose temporarily
- Increase drainage support
- Add extra binder dose
- Rest more

### Fat-Soluble Vitamins (A, D, E, K)

**Take WITH dinner (fatty meal):**
- Vitamin A: 5,000-10,000 IU
- Vitamin D3: 2,000-5,000 IU (test levels)
- Vitamin E: 400 IU mixed tocopherols
- Vitamin K2: 100-200 mcg

**Why with dinner?**
- CSM taken before dinner binds less of these vitamins
- Fat in meal enhances absorption
- Maximizes time separation from CSM doses

### Additional Key Supplements

**Morning (2+ hours from CSM):**
- B-complex vitamin
- Vitamin C (1000-2000mg)
- Minerals (zinc, selenium, copper)
- Probiotics

**Evening (with dinner):**
- Magnesium (400-800mg)
- Omega-3 fatty acids
- CoQ10 if taking

Many patients struggle to get CSM prescribed locally. Telehealth options:

## Troubleshooting Guide

### "I Can't Tolerate Even ¼ Scoop"

**Try:**
1. Start with ⅛ scoop once daily
2. Mix with small amount apple juice
3. Take with food initially
4. Switch to Welchol
5. Ensure drainage pathways open
6. Check for SIBO/gut issues

### "I'm Not Improving After 4 Weeks"

**Consider:**
1. Still being exposed to mold
2. Need higher dose
3. Need to add antifungals
4. Other infections present
5. Drainage pathways blocked
6. Wrong diagnosis

### "Extreme Fatigue After Starting"

**Indicates:**
- Herxheimer reaction
- Too much too fast
- Need more drainage support

**Action:**
- Reduce dose by half
- Increase water intake
- Add infrared sauna
- Consider activated charcoal between doses
- Rest more

## Long-Term Considerations

### Duration of Treatment

**Typical Timeline:**
- Minimum: 8-12 weeks
- Average: 3-6 months
- Severe cases: 12+ months
- Genetically susceptible (HLA-DR): Often longer

### After Stopping Binders

**Maintenance Protocol:**
- Continue drainage support
- Monthly "pulse" of binders (1 week/month)
- Quarterly VCS testing
- Maintain mold-free environment
- Consider preventive binders if re-exposed

## Next Steps

Once you've been on therapeutic dose CSM (1 scoop 3-4x daily) for at least 2-4 weeks and are tolerating it well, you may be ready to consider adding antifungals (Module 05). However, many patients do well with binders alone for several months before adding additional interventions.

Remember: **Slow and steady wins this race.** It's better to take 6 months and feel good throughout than rush and create severe Herx reactions.
